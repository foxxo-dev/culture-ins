All Similar, and Different.

We investigated the different cultures and nationalities of our classmates and later interviewed them. The topics that we interviewed them about were Personal Appearance, Diet, and Greetings. After getting our answers we used them to answer our primary question. Which is about the striking similarities and differences of our classmates and their cultures.

Claim

In my opinion, there are a lot of similarities between different cultures, but there are also differences that would be hard to find similarities in as they are completely dependent on culture. In my opinion, the way people greet their friends is quite similar. Our evidence to back this up is that they all say Hi/Hello to each other and give each other high fives which is quite expected. Another similarity is how they dress to each other on a casual day. They all wear jeans and a T-shirt which is still basic but similar.

Similarities

Personal Appearance

We’ve noticed that people who come from South Korea, Poland, and Belarus dress quite similarly. All of them casually wear a pair of jeans and a T-shirt. They don’t have a huge preference but prefer to wear Nike or other bigger brands. They like to dress nicely for formal occasions.

Diet

At home, both Poles and South Koreans eat different types of soup. While South Koreans like to eat more traditional foods which our research did state.

Greetings

All 3 nationalities we interviewed greet each other quite similarly to their friends. They all say Hi or Hello and then talk with each other. They give high-fives to each other when they meet. Culture grams stated similarly to this.

Differences

Personal Appearance

On formal occasions, they’re quite different though. Polish people prefer to wear suits to formal occasions while Belarussians like to wear something new and more formal but not a suit. In both Poland and Belarus, they don’t need to wear school uniforms however in South Korea they have to wear a uniform, even on warm days.

Diet

The person I interviewed for Belarus was quite different from my research as for breakfast they eat different things. The person for Belarus prefers fruits and vegetables for lunch which is only a bit similar to our research.

Greetings

However, when greeting adults they are quite different. Polish people normally kiss each other on the cheek if they're the opposite gender and then shake hands. Belarussian people would say Good Morning or something else depending on the time of day, and then shake hands. The South Korean person I interviewed says that if they’re greeting an adult they will bow, and if it’s a family member they would bow and shake hands.
